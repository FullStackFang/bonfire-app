BONFIRE THE SYSTEM · PRODUCT THESIS · JUN 2026

Loneliness isn’t a discovery problem.
It’s a repetition problem.

Bonfire turns “I’m free” into people next to you — a real-time presence layer for spontaneous and recurring co-presence with the people near you. Not a feed, not an events platform, not a planner. Connection forms through repeated, low-stakes co-presence with the same people in the same places. Every other app treats that as discovery, content, or messaging. Bonfire treats it as behavior, and engineers the repetition.

01

The reframe & the north star

The behavioral move

Other apps optimize for the first meeting. Bonfire optimizes for the tenth — the same faces, the same place, until a random hang quietly becomes a crew.

3× North star: repeat co-presence. The share of people who are physically with the same crew 3+ times a month. Not signups, not RSVPs, not reach — bodies, together, again.
02

The DNA

Six experience principles everything obeys. If a feature breaks one, it doesn’t ship.

Presence is a statement, not a question.
No Going / Maybe, no RSVP. You broadcast where you are. There’s no rejection surface.
The app does the work; you show up.
Coordination, recruiting and scheduling are the app’s job, not yours. Fewer taps, never more.
No feed, no chat.
Nothing to scroll and decide. Real conversation stays in the group chat you already use.
Status, not surveillance.
Self-reported presence, never live GPS. You say here — the phone never says it for you.
Hyperlocal.
Closest-viable wins. For recurring things, convenience is retention — walkable beats marginally-closer-by-car.
Open to value, never a wall.
A link opens straight to the live thing. The app is an upgrade, never a gate.
03

Two objects, two layers

The whole product reduces to two atomic objects — the pulse and the crew — arranged as a ceiling and a floor on top of one shared graph.

The spontaneous ceiling

The pulse

A live micro-event — “USA vs Germany at Smithfield, 5pm,” “jog the West Side at 7.” Spontaneous, ephemeral, as high as a given week wants to go. It rides the group chat and opens to value for anyone who taps.

↓   both run on the same infrastructure   ↓
The guaranteed floor

The ritual — a recurring pulse

A pulse with “repeat” on, pointed at the same people. A system-owned cadence with the place and the gathering pre-handled, so there’s always a floor under your week even when nobody feels like sparking something.

The crew The durable group that forms when the same people keep showing up — held together by the “would hang again” graph beneath both layers.

The pulse, end to end

The core workflow — one pulse’s whole lifecycle. Prototyped

01
Spark / express demand

Start a pulse (activity + place + time, nearby suggestions pre-filled) or just say “tennis, near me” and let the system place you. Create-vs-join is invisible. Under ten seconds.

02
Seed

The pulse link drops into your group chat as a live card. Nobody installs anything.

03
Gather

People tap in and set status. It reads as forming and alive at 3–4 (“3 in, just getting going”), never “1 going, nobody coming.” On spikes, the card re-surfaces in the chat.

04

The hero moment: who’s here, who’s → on the way with an ETA, a freeform note (“got us a table, come find me”). Real-world co-presence happens.

05
Wind-down

A quiet wrap — “that’s a wrap, 6 of you made it” — which doubles as your co-presence record. A TTL closes it.

06
Afterglow

The link leaves a lightweight trace and the one-tap “again?” fires. A random hang becomes the seed for the next one.

06

The ritual — the forced floor

The pulse is the spontaneous ceiling; the ritual is the guaranteed floor. Spec only

Borrow Timeleft’s forcing function — a system-owned cadence with the place and the gathering pre-handled — but point it at the same people, so repetition builds a real crew. (Timeleft’s flaw is reshuffling strangers.) A recurring pulse is just a pulse with “repeat” on: activity + place + cadence + rough headcount, plus one toggle — “keep it just us” or “open up if short.” Then it runs itself.

  • The app owns the cadence and auto-spawns a fresh pulse each cycle. Nobody creates or remembers it.
  • The place is a default or a small rotation — no “where should we go.”
  • People are summoned opt-out, not opt-in: “tennis tomorrow, the usual at 7, you in?” Default is you’re expected; you act only to bail. For settled regulars, even that defaults to presumed-in.
  • Graceful death. If turnout decays for a few weeks it auto-pauses and asks “still want this?” — rather than nag.
07

Matching & placement

How groups form when there’s no crew yet — never a public board you browse. Spec only

The category is public (tennis); the placement is private and curated (you’re slotted into a small gated game by fit). That’s the line between Bonfire and Meetup.

  • Concentric, demand-gated expansion. Rings open only if the pulse is under its size target: your friends → your network → matched users nearby. Recruit strangers only when you actually need bodies. A pulse that fills with friends never goes public.
  • Hyperlocal-first. Proximity is the primary signal — the only one available at cold-start. “Closest” means closest-viable: fill the nearest existing game before spawning a new one. The radius flexes with density (why Cornell and one NYC neighborhood are the beachheads).
  • Curated, not uncurated — the edge over Timeleft. Match on proximity + fit (skill, vibe, age) now, and actual attendance history later — the show-up reliability signal Timeleft structurally can’t have.

  • One tap, after a real hang. No notes, no CRM — the system already knows you were there together.
  • Scoped for free. You played tennis, so “again” is automatically about tennis — it routes that person to tennis pulses, not your book club.
  • Intensity from repetition. Keep tapping “again,” or just keep showing up together, and intent escalates. Behavior overwrites the tap.
  • Mutual is a green light. Both tap “again” and you lock into a crew. One-sided stays private; unrequited interest is never exposed.

This graph powers everything: it’s the friend-first ring; it fires the rekindle nudge (tapped “again” but haven’t seen them in a month); and it makes the ritual emerge — enough mutual “again”s for one activity and the system offers “make this a standing Wednesday thing?”

09

Pool → crew — membership that manages itself

A forming group runs off one signal: weekly in/out plus who actually shows. Spec only

  • Membership is emergent, not administered. Show up = you’re in. Drift = your seat quietly reopens. Come back = welcomed. Nobody formally joins or quits.
  • Replacement is a feature early, a bug late. While it’s a loose pool, churn freely to find the right core. Once a core overlaps enough it crystallizes into a crew and locks — replacement goes rare and explicit.
  • Missed-one is not gone. A single absence never evicts — sub a guest for that session, keep the standing seat. Sub in, don’t swap out.
  • Transparency scales with bonding. A pool can be silent; a crew can’t. New faces get a visible welcome; a lapsing regular gets a gentle “still want your spot?” before the seat reopens. Never silently cut a regular.
  • The creator’s ongoing job is zero. Bonfire is the permanent organizer.
10

Contacts, enrichment & privacy

Consent is the architecture, not a setting bolted on after. Spec only

  • Enriched contacts are a byproduct of use, not a CRM chore. The durable record is who you actually show up with; manual notes are an optional cold-start shortcut.
  • Phone-number enrichment normalizes and hashes numbers, then matches against the user base — the same contact discovery Signal and WhatsApp use.
  • You only surface what you choose to surface (set at onboarding). Your private notes about others stay yours — never shown to them, never used to match them. Discoverability is per-user and rate-limited against bulk enumeration. Defaults lean a notch more private than neutral, because the inference is sensitive.
11

The behavioral spine

Bonfire runs a behavior-change loop on your social life — but the change is environmental, not willpower. Noom logs your eating to nudge better habits; Bonfire logs one bit per real hang (“again?”) and uses it to engineer more of the right ones. You don’t read lessons or summon discipline — you show up, tap “again,” and the system quietly rebuilds your week around the people you click with.

The heavy emotional message — loneliness is real, it’s not your fault, it’s solvable — lives in the movement brand (Rage Against The Social Machine) and the funnel, never in the daily app. The app itself stays light: your people, Thursday tennis, the relief of a full Friday. Belonging, not the deficit.

The whole arc, in one line

Tap a link or install, see your people are out, show up, “again?”, the same people nearby every week, a crew — you’re not alone on a Friday.
