01

The link is the seed

Bonfire’s core mechanic and its growth loop are the same gesture. A pulse is dropped into a group chat people already use, it opens to value for everyone — app or not — and the lightest possible join puts a curious tapper on the list. The app exists only to make the recurring signal louder (push) and persistent (every crew in one place).

02

The vocabulary

Two atomic objects carry the product — the pulse and the crew. These prototypes use the first four; the last two are the system layers above them. in these flows · spec only

Pulse One ephemeral “who’s around” link for a single moment — an activity, a place, a time. No RSVP, no replies, no chat. A live scoreboard, not an event.
Crew The durable group that forms when the same people keep showing up. The app keeps every crew in one place; the chat keeps the conversation.
Status The only thing you broadcast. here → on my way heading out
Headcount The number, set as a typographic event — 7 in · 5 here · 2 on the way. Warmth is always earned by real bodies, never inflated.
Ritual A pulse with “repeat” on, pointed at the same people — the guaranteed floor under your week. Auto-spawns each cycle; summons opt-out, not opt-in.
“Again” One tap after a real hang. Mutual “again” is a platonic double opt-in — the signal that turns a one-off pulse into a locked crew.

The three flows

A

Pulse Link — the core loop

In-app · create a pulse and read the room
Open prototype →

The heart of the product. Someone in the group chat drops a pulse; tapping it opens the live view — a running headcount and who’s here, on the way, or heading out.

The create sheet is deliberately tiny: pick an activity, the nearest place is filled in for you, choose a time, drop the link. The conversation never leaves the group chat — the link is purely the scoreboard. As the night fills, you flip your own status and others see it move in real time.

Web Flow — the appless on-ramp

The growth path. A recipient with no app taps the same link and it opens straight to the live view in mobile Safari — open to value, never a download gate.

Onboarding — the app path

Fresh install · funnel hard toward a crew, because the app is empty without one

For people who install. Account signup is name + phone (SMS code, no password), then notifications framed as the payoff — one ping the moment your crew goes out.

The critical step is “get into a crew”: paste an invite link, start a crew, or pull friends from contacts — so no one dead-ends in an empty app. Lands on Home: crews and any live pulses, with a to start one.

04

How they fit together

One pulse link is shared once and serves three audiences at once. The creator and existing app users live in Flow A. A friend without the app falls into Flow B and may never need more than the web. Anyone who wants the recurring signal crosses into Flow C — and the soft nudge at the end of B is the bridge.

Read top to bottom, that’s the funnel: see → join → come back → install. Each step is opt-in and reversible; value always arrives before any ask.

05

What these cover — and what’s next

These three flows are the spontaneous slice: the pulse lifecycle and the two on-ramps onto it. That’s the ceiling of the product — the high-energy, “who’s out right now” moment. The earlier draft of this page flagged a v1/v2 tension; the current thesis resolves it. The appless link stays (gating moves to placement, not a wall on the link); a recurring pulse is just a pulse with repeat on; and the harsher “mortal fire / collective blame” mechanic is softened into graceful auto-pause. Both app and web are first-class by design.

What the prototypes don’t yet show is the rest of the system — the floor that turns a fun night into a standing crew. Four layers are specified but not drawn:

The ritual nextThe recurring floor — a self-running cadence that summons the same crew opt-out, not opt-in, and auto-pauses gracefully when turnout fades.
Matching & placement nextExpress demand → invisible routing into a small, gated, curated, hyperlocal game. Public category, private placement — never a board you browse.
The “again” engine nextOne tap after a real hang. Mutual “again” is the green light that turns one-off pulses into a locked crew — and makes the ritual emerge.
Crew lifecycle nextPool → crystallize → lock → graceful death. Membership manages itself off one weekly in/out signal, with zero ongoing work from the creator.
